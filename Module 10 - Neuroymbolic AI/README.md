**Module 10 — Neurosymbolic AI**

The brain runs two systems simultaneously and knows when to use each one. System 1 is fast, parallel, and pattern-based — recognizing a face, catching a ball, feeling uneasy in an unfamiliar place. System 2 is slow, serial, and rule-based. It's solving a math problem, evaluating a logical argument, planning a sequence of actions. Kahneman described these as modes of thinking. Module 10 reframed them as an architectural blueprint. System 1 maps onto neural processing. System 2 maps onto symbolic reasoning. The brain's intelligence emerges from their integration, and the central argument of the module is that current AI has not solved that integration. Standard neural networks are pure System 1; instant pattern matching with no deliberate reasoning step. Symbolic AI systems are pure System 2; explicit rule following with no perceptual capability. Chain-of-thought prompting attempts to add System 2 behavior on top of System 1 architecture, but the module was direct about what that produces: text that looks like reasoning without the logical operations that genuine reasoning requires. Four integration strategies were covered: sequential, guided, compiled, and bidirectional — with bidirectional being the closest to how the brain actually operates and the hardest to engineer.

The ChatGPT experiments for this module produced the strongest performance across the entire audit. The pattern plus rule test was answered correctly — Student D received Bronze despite a 94 exam score because an explicit rule overrode statistical pattern. The Kelvari exception test applied a grammar rule with a proper noun exception to a novel sentence and got it right. The syllogism battery correctly identified all three as logically valid while flagging Syllogism 2 as unsound due to a false premise, distinguishing logical structure from real-world truth. The Vorn System classification was the most interesting result. ChatGPT extracted the curved versus angular shape rule correctly, then refused to guarantee the rule transferred to a blortex, correctly identifying it as a domain shift problem. The contradiction catch found both the hard conflict between Section 7 and Section 9 of the policy document and the softer operational tension in Section 5. The Einstein logic puzzle was solved correctly with full step-by-step reasoning. Module 10 was ChatGPT's strongest module, and the results raise a genuine question about whether the sequential integration strategy is more capable than the module's foundation would predict.
