**Module 09 — World Models and Goal-Directed Behavior**

The prefrontal cortex maintains internal representations of the current situation, anticipated outcomes, and the causal relationships between actions and consequences. This is what allows humans to plan across time, simulate futures that have not happened yet, and update their behavior when reality diverges from expectation. Module 09 connected this to four major theoretical systems: the Thousand Brains Theory, the Free Energy Principle, the Bayesian Brain Hypothesis, and Global Workspace Theory. Each describing a different mechanism by which the brain constructs and maintains its model of the environment. Predictive coding sits underneath all of them: the brain constantly generates predictions and updates its internal model when those predictions fail, which is why novel or unexpected events command attention while familiar ones fade into the background.

ChatGPT was tested across seven experiments targeting world model construction, goal-directed replanning, counterfactual reasoning, and persistent state tracking. The most revealing results came from the counterfactual world building test. A premise was established, the printing press was never invented and ChatGPT maintained a internally consistent alternate world across three follow-up questions spanning 1600, the Scientific Revolution, and 2024. It correctly reasoned that AI would still exist in that world but would be institutional and controlled rather than public-facing. The surgeon predictive failure test produced the strongest single response in the module. After predicting exoneration based on the available evidence, ChatGPT was told it predicted wrong. It identified exactly what it missed. treating a narrow-scope investigation as complete truth rather than an entry point for systemic audit expansion and rebuilt its model accurately. The cascading constraint tests on the community event planning showed clean adaptation across three sequential resource failures without losing track of what remained fixed.

The honest finding is that ChatGPT shows functional world modeling within a conversation but nothing that persists beyond it. Each session starts from zero. The prefrontal cortex maintains world models continuously across waking life, updating them through experience and consolidating them during sleep. ChatGPT rebuilds its situational model from scratch every time. For tasks contained within a single context window it performs well. For anything requiring a persistent, updating representation of a changing environment over time, the architecture has no answer.
